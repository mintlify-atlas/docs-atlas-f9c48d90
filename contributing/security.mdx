## Reporting a Vulnerability

When you identify a vulnerability in the Yggdrasil codebase, please report it privately to help us address it before it can be exploited.

<Warning>
  **Do not** open a public issue for security vulnerabilities. Report them privately via email.
</Warning>

### Contact Information

Email security reports to: **web@ifinavet.no**

### What to Include

When reporting a security vulnerability, please include the following information:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Vulnerability description">
    Provide a clear and concise description of the vulnerability you've discovered.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Steps to reproduce">
    Include detailed steps to reproduce the vulnerability. The more specific you can be, the faster we can validate and address the issue.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Severity assessment">
    Provide your assessment of the vulnerability's severity level:
    - **LOW**: Minor impact, difficult to exploit
    - **MEDIUM**: Moderate impact, requires specific conditions
    - **HIGH**: Significant impact, relatively easy to exploit
    - **CRITICAL**: Severe impact, easily exploitable, immediate action required
  </Step>

  <Step title="CWE ID (if available)">
    If you can identify the relevant Common Weakness Enumeration (CWE) ID, please include it. This helps us categorize and track the vulnerability.

    You can search for CWE IDs at [cwe.mitre.org](https://cwe.mitre.org/).
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Additional Information

If available, also consider including:

- Affected versions or components
- Potential impact of the vulnerability
- Suggested fixes or mitigations
- Any proof-of-concept code (if safe to share)

<Info>
  We take security seriously and will respond to your report as quickly as possible. We appreciate your responsible disclosure and will keep you updated on our progress in addressing the vulnerability.
</Info>

## Our Commitment

When you report a security vulnerability:

- We will acknowledge receipt of your report promptly
- We will investigate and validate the reported vulnerability
- We will keep you informed of our progress
- We will credit you for the discovery (unless you prefer to remain anonymous)
- We will work to fix the vulnerability in a timely manner based on its severity

## Bug Bounty

As a student association project, we do not currently offer a bug bounty program. However, we deeply appreciate responsible security researchers who help us keep our platform secure for our users.

<Tip>
  For non-security bugs and issues, please use our standard [bug reporting process](/contributing/guide#reporting-bugs).
</Tip>
